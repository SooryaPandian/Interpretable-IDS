Attack: Reconnaissance
Definition:
Reconnaissance identifies hosts, ports, services, and security posture before active exploitation.

Analyst Interpretation Notes:
- Reconnaissance is discovery-oriented and usually low-impact by itself.
- Recon-class evidence should focus on breadth of probing (many hosts/ports) rather than exploit payload depth.
- If the event is highly targeted with exploit-like payload behavior, classify as exploit instead of recon.

Common Indicators in Network Traffic:
- Sequential port scanning behavior
- Host discovery sweeps and DNS enumeration
- Many short-lived connection attempts
- Broad destination spread with low per-destination interaction depth
- Service fingerprinting patterns (banner/metadata probing)

Potential Impact:
- Increased probability of targeted follow-on attacks

Validation Checks for SOC Teams:
- Measure destination cardinality and scan velocity across the source entity
- Compare against approved vulnerability scanners and maintenance windows
- Verify if probing pattern aligns with known benign inventory tooling

Containment and Response:
- Block repeated scanners and suspicious netblocks
- Monitor critical assets for follow-up intrusion attempts
- Review firewall exposure and segmentation controls
- Tighten scan detection thresholds for exposed subnets
- Add watchlist alerts for recurring recon source fingerprints
