Class: Normal / Benign Traffic
Definition:
Normal traffic follows expected protocol behavior, service usage patterns, and baseline host communication profiles.

Typical Indicators:
- Feature values remain within baseline ranges for the environment
- No strong malicious signatures in payload or flow-level attributes
- No coordinated scan/flood/exploit progression across pipeline stages

How To Interpret In IDS Context:
- Binary model indicates low attack probability
- Multiclass attack stage is skipped or not confidently triggered
- SHAP highlights may still exist, but contribution magnitudes are usually low or balanced

Operational Recommendation:
- Treat as benign unless corroborating telemetry (EDR, SIEM, firewall, auth logs) suggests otherwise
- Continue passive monitoring and baseline drift tracking
